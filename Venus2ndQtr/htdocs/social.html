Social Life: Why does it matter?

As humans, social interaction is essential to every aspect of our health. Research shows that having a strong network of support or strong community bonds fosters both emotional and physical health and is an important component of adult life.Social involvement is also important as we age.

Maintaining our social health healthy brings many benefits. Some are listed below:

1. Gives you more opportunities.

2. Gives you better life quality.

3. Improves your dating life.

4. Improving your self-confidence.

5. You will have many companions.

Socialisation is an important part of human development. It starts as early as birth with skin-to-skin contact and endless cuddles.Sharing a pleasant connection with others can trigger the release of stress-reducing hormones.

Social life is so important that it sharpens us so that we can cope to other people. Social life doesn't only means on how you bond with others but also means on how you care about them also.

"A healthy social life is found only when in the mirror of each soul the whole community finds its reflection, and when in the whole community the virtue of each one is living."

-Rudolf Steiner
